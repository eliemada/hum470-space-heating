## Key Decision: 3-Factor vs 5-Factor LMDI

**Decision: Keep 3-factor LMDI.**

The 5-factor identity `E = P × (D/P) × (A/D) × Σ(S_s × e_s)` requires annual time-series of dwellings, total floor area, heating shares, and specific energy by fuel — most of which are interpolated or missing before 2012.

**Strategy**: 3-factor LMDI `E = P × HDD × I` as quantitative backbone (zero residual, all real data), then qualitatively sub-decompose the "intensity" residual into envelope improvement, fuel switching, and floor area growth using discrete data points.
