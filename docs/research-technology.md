## 1. Current Heating Mix (2024)

| Energy carrier | Share of buildings | Trend |
|---|---|---|
| Heating oil | ~35% | Declining (was ~65% in 1990) |
| Heat pumps | ~20% (28% for single-family) | Rising fast (was ~4% in 2000) |
| Natural gas | ~17% | Flat/declining |
| Wood/biomass | ~10-12% | Stable |
| District heating | ~4% | Growing slowly |
| Electric resistance | ~10% (remainder) | Declining |

**Key inflection points:**
- 1973-79: Oil crises — first heat pump interest
- 1990: "Energie 2000" program — HP sales grow from 2-3k/yr to 20k/yr by 2007
- 2021-22: European gas crisis — accelerated oil-to-HP conversions
- 2023: Record HP installations (~50k/yr)
- 2024: **Reversal** — HP installations -30%, gas/oil installations +12% (cost pressures)

## 2. Heat Pumps — Detailed

### Penetration
- ~450,000 units installed (end 2024)
- 75% of new buildings use heat pumps
- 84% of replaced heating systems in 2024 chose heat pumps (via Gebaudeprogramm)

### Types & COP

| Type | Market share | COP (radiators) | COP (underfloor) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Air-to-water | ~68% of revenue | 2.7 | 3.5 |
| Ground-source (brine) | ~32% of revenue | 3.6 | 4.5 |

### Costs (CHF)

| System | Single-family (~150 m2) | Multi-family |
|---|---|---|
| Air-to-water HP | 35,000-45,000 | 100,000-400,000+ |
| Ground-source HP | 50,000-70,000 | Higher (drilling) |
| Gas/oil boiler | 10,000-20,000 | - |
| Payback period | 10-15 years | - |

### Cantonal Leaders
- **Basel-Stadt**: Grants ~CHF 30,000 for ground-source HP (40% capex)
- **Zug**: Grants from CHF 25,000
- **Zurich**: Up to 40% capex grants
- **Geneva**: Up to CHF 40,000 for district heating + HP support

### Barriers for Multi-Family Buildings
- Many older MFH need >60C supply temp (HP optimal at 35-55C)
- Urban density: noise regulations, no drilling space
- **Split incentive**: Owner pays capex, tenant gets opex savings
- Strata decisions require majority vote → slow
- 87% of HPs sold are <20 kW (too small for MFH)

## 3. Building Standards Evolution

### MuKEn Versions

| Version | Year | Key requirement |
|---|---|---|
| First model ordinance | 1992 | Rational energy use |
| MuKEn 2000 | 2000 | Tightened thermal insulation |
| MuKEn 2008 | 2008 | + renewables + low-carbon heat |
| MuKEn 2014 | 2015 | "Energy hub building"; fossil heat-free direction |
| MuKEn 2025 | 2025 | Climate-neutrality focus |

**MuKEn 2014 adoption**: ~22/26 cantons as of 2023

### Heating Energy Limits Over Time

| Period | Approx. heating energy limit (new build) |
|---|---|
| Pre-1975 (no norm) | 200-300+ kWh/m2/yr |
| 1975 (first SIA 180/380) | ~160-200 kWh/m2/yr |
| MuKEn 2000 / SIA 380/1:2001 | ~80-100 kWh/m2/yr |
| MuKEn 2008 / SIA 380/1:2007 | ~60 kWh/m2/yr |
| MuKEn 2014 / SIA 380/1:2016 | **16 kWh/m2/yr** (new) / 24 kWh/m2/yr (renovation) |

### MuKEn 2014 U-Values

| Component | U-value limit [W/(m2K)] |
|---|---|
| Walls, roof, floor | 0.17 |
| Windows | 1.0 |
| Exterior doors | 1.2 |
| Roller shutter boxes | 0.5 |

### Fossil Heating Replacement Rule (MuKEn 2014)
When replacing a fossil system: at least **10% of heating demand** must come from renewables (or 10% demand reduction). Some cantons go further (full ban: Neuchatel, Basel-Stadt, Glarus).

## 4. Renovation Rates — The Bottleneck

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Current annual renovation rate | 0.9-1.4% |
| Rate needed for Energy Strategy 2050 | ~2-2.2% |
| Rate needed for net-zero 2050 | ~3% |
| Buildings built before 1980 | ~2/3 of stock (~1M poorly insulated) |
| Time to renovate all at current pace | ~100 years |

**Depth problem**: Most renovations are **shallow** (single measure like window replacement), not **deep** (comprehensive envelope + systems). 2024 Gebaudeprogramm: CHF 228M for heating systems vs CHF 86M for comprehensive system renovations.

## 5. Carbon Pricing & Policy

### CO2 Levy History

| Period | Rate (CHF/tCO2) |
|---|---|
| 2008-2009 | 12 |
| 2010-2013 | 36 |
| 2014-2015 | 60 |
| 2016-2017 | 84 |
| 2018-2021 | 96 |
| 2022-present | **120** |
| 2025-2030 | **Frozen at 120** (no increase planned) |

- Applies to: heating oil, natural gas, coal
- Does NOT apply to: electricity, motor fuels, biomass
- Revenue: ~CHF 1.2 billion/year
- 1/3 goes to Gebaudeprogramm (max CHF 450M/yr), rest redistributed to population

### Gebaudeprogramm (Buildings Programme)
- **2010-2024 cumulative**: ~CHF 4.1 billion in subsidies
- **Energy savings**: 12 billion kWh
- **CO2 avoided**: ~3.5 million tonnes
- **2024**: 25,633 fossil systems replaced (84% to heat pumps)
- **2025 WARNING**: Parliament cut subsidies by 25%; possible further cuts

### Cantonal Fossil Heating Bans

| Canton | Status |
|---|---|
| Zurich | Ban approved by voters (63%), 2022 |
| Basel-Stadt | Effective ban in force |
| Basel-Landschaft | Ban effective 2026 |
| Geneva | Virtually impossible in most cases |
| Glarus | Ban on renovation |
| Neuchatel | Ban on renovation |

### Key Legislation
- **CO2 Act** (revised, in force Jan 2025): -50% GHG by 2030 vs 1990. No federal fossil heating ban.
- **Climate Protection Act** (referendum June 2023, 59% yes): Net zero by 2050, -75% by 2040.
- **Swiss ETS**: Does NOT cover buildings (only large industry, power, aviation).
- **Post-2030**: Consulting on expanded ETS potentially covering buildings (like EU ETS2).

### Households

|---|---|---|
| Total households | 4.1M | ~4.9M |
| Average size | 2.18 persons | 2.10 persons |

Trend: More single-person households, smaller families.

### GDP
- 2024 nominal: ~CHF 936 billion
- Long-run growth: ~1.8% p.a.
- GDP per capita: ~USD 103,670

### Floor Area
- Per person: 44 m2 (2000) → 46 m2 (2020), trending to ~48-50 m2 by 2050
- Total residential EBF: ~480-550 million m2
- New construction adds ~4-5 million m2/year
- Demolition rate: only 0.1-0.2% of stock/year

### Heating Degree Days (HDD)
- Trend: **-2.5% per decade** (1981-2017)
- Swiss reference: ~3,400 HDD (historical Zurich); current actual ~2,800-3,000
- Switzerland has warmed ~2.9C above pre-industrial (2-3x global average)
- Normalization formula: weather-adjusted energy = actual energy x (reference HDD / actual year HDD)

## 7. Energieperspektiven 2050+ (Official Projections)

- Heat demand: **86.7 TWh (2019) → 58.6 TWh by 2050** (-33%)
- Fossil heating: **essentially phased out** in net-zero scenario
- Dominant tech: heat pumps + district heating + solar thermal
- Key challenge: winter electricity deficit of ~10 TWh/year by 2050
- Climate change itself reduces heating demand by 33-44% by 2050-2100
