## Slide 5 (Three Mechanisms) — MAJOR CHANGES

**Reorder the three mechanisms. Fuel switching is now #1 (computed from data).**

### Old (delete):
1. Building envelope ~45% (-30,300 TJ)
2. Fuel switching ~35% (-23,600 TJ)
3. Behavioural ~20% (-13,500 TJ)

### New (replace with):

**1. Fuel switching to heat pumps — ~51% of intensity effect (-34,700 TJ)**
- Key Driver: COP 3-4 means 60-75% less final energy vs. oil/gas
- Oil share of heated floor area: 59.9% (2000) to 23.9% (2024); HPs: 3.6% to 28.9%
- Computed from fleet effective energy multiplier (Prognos Tab.13 x system efficiencies)
- Independently corroborated by Gebaudeprogramm Jahresbericht 2024 (Haustechnik = 62% of programme energy savings)

**2. Building envelope improvements — ~33% of intensity effect (-22,700 TJ)**
- Key Driver: U-values improved from 0.8 to 0.15 W/m2K in new builds and retrofits
- Renovation rate: 1.1%/yr of buildings; ~40% of stock now at post-2000 standards
- Derived as residual (2/3 of non-fuel-switching effect, based on stock-turnover rate)

**3. Behavioural and controls — ~16% of intensity effect (-11,200 TJ)**
- Residual: smart thermostats, lower setpoints (~21C to 20C), building automation
- Partly supported by Prognos household survey data
