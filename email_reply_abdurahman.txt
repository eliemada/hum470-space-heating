Good news: we've already addressed the main concern. The intensity sub-decomposition (the split between envelope, fuel switching, and behaviour) is no longer taken from literature. We now compute the fuel-switching share directly from our own data:

- We track the fleet's "effective energy multiplier" year by year using floor-area shares by heating system (Prognos Tabelle 13) combined with system efficiencies (COP for heat pumps, combustion efficiency for boilers).
- This multiplier dropped ~20% from 2000 to 2024 as oil (1/0.90 = 1.11 final energy per useful heat unit) was replaced by heat pumps (1/3.5 = 0.29).
- We weight each year's contribution using the LMDI log-mean weights, maintaining consistency with the main decomposition.
- The envelope + behavioural share is derived as the residual, split 2:1 based on stock-turnover estimates.

The updated results:
- Fuel switching: ~51% (-34,700 TJ) — up from the old literature-based ~35%
- Building envelope: ~33% (-22,700 TJ) — down from ~45%
- Behavioural: ~16% (-11,200 TJ) — down from ~20%

We also cross-validated this against the Gebäudeprogramm Jahresbericht 2024, which independently reports that Haustechnik (heating system replacement) accounts for 62% of programme energy savings vs. 19% for Wärmedämmung (insulation) — consistent with our finding that fuel switching dominates.

The total -67,366 TJ intensity effect was always our own LMDI computation — we've now added an explicit counterfactual calculation in the report to make this clearer (E_counterfactual = P_2024 x (A/P)_2024 x HDD_2024 x I_2000).
